# User agreement launch checklist

The deployable draft is `deploy/terms.html`, version 2026-07-14. It is not ready for production acceptance until all bracketed placeholders are replaced and counsel approves it.

## Required owner decisions

- Operate through a properly formed, maintained, and insured legal entity; do not name the individual owner as the contracting party unless counsel directs it.
- Add exact legal entity name, mailing address, support/privacy email, governing state and county.
- Select an arbitration administrator and add its current consumer rules, fees, hearing location, and legally required notices—or remove arbitration after legal advice.
- Decide launch geography. Geofence unsupported jurisdictions until their privacy, dating-service, safety-notice, automatic-renewal, and consumer-contract rules are reviewed.
- Publish a process for privacy requests, law-enforcement requests, breach response, content preservation, minor reports, and emergency escalation.
- Obtain cyber/general liability and media/professional coverage appropriate for a dating platform.

## Why this cannot eliminate owner risk

Contracts cannot waive every statutory duty, regulatory action, gross negligence or intentional misconduct claim, and anyone may still file a lawsuit even if a defense ultimately succeeds. Liability protection depends on the operating entity, corporate formalities, insurance, actual security/privacy practices, accurate representations, and enforceable presentation/acceptance—not wording alone.

## Legal research flags

- Illinois expressly regulates compensated computer dating services and makes noncompliant contracts void; assess applicability before charging Illinois members.
- The FTC recommends privacy-by-design, data minimization, express consent for sensitive data, reasonable security, and honoring every privacy promise.
- Because signup asks for date of birth, reject and delete underage submissions immediately; the product is designed only for adults 18+.
- Do not claim comprehensive background checks, guaranteed safety, guaranteed moderation, or absolute security unless the operation can substantiate those claims.
